Self-qualification FAQ

Use these answers to filter your evaluation before engaging sales — most teams know their tier from revenue and throughput alone.

How are “Revenue” and “Throughput” measured for the free tier?

If your company makes over $1,000,000 USD in gross annual revenue, or your production applications serialize or deserialize more than 100 GB of .nxb payloads across your network lanes in a single calendar month, your standard BSL 1.1 allowance expires. You must step into a paid commercial agreement.

Are our client applications bound by the BSL license?

No. All client-side readers and writers (nyxis-drivers) are licensed under the ultra-permissive MIT license. You can embed them in edge applications, mobile devices, IoT hardware, or client browsers with absolutely zero copyleft or compliance friction. Only the core compilation engine and private performance daemons require a commercial seat.

Which tier do I need if I only exceed one BSL gate?

Either gate triggers commercial licensing. You must comply with both the revenue cap and the monthly throughput cap to remain on the free tier. Crossing either threshold moves you to Enterprise Core or Principal Scale depending on scale and extension requirements.
